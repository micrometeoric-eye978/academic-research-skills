## 📌 About this project

Academic research requires time and focus. This tool helps you find papers and refine your writing. It connects to databases like CNKI, ScienceDirect, and Google Scholar to save you hours of manual searching. You can use it to locate specific research on topics like neural rendering or to draft introductions for your own work.

## ⚙️ System requirements

This application runs on Windows 10 and Windows 11. Your computer needs at least 8 GB of RAM and 500 MB of free storage space. Ensure you have an active internet connection to communicate with research databases.

## 📖 How to navigate the interface

The application landing page shows four main buttons. Each button corresponds to a core research skill.

### Searching for papers
Click the Search button to begin. Type your keywords into the search bar. You can toggle between databases like CNKI or Google Scholar. The results appear in a list below the search bar. Click any title to see more details or to copy the direct link.

### Tracking citations
Use the Citing Papers tool to see who references a specific study. Paste the URL of a paper into the box. The tool scans Google Scholar and Web of Science to return a list of works that cite that paper. This helps you track how a specific field of study evolves over time.

### Downloading documents
When you find a paper on ScienceDirect, copy the URL. Paste it into the download tool in the application. The system retrieves the PDF for you. It saves the file to your documents folder in a new directory named Research_Downloads.

### Writing support
The writing tool helps you draft research content. Select the Nature-style introduction option. Input your research topic and key findings. The tool creates a structured draft that follows common academic formatting. Use this as a starting point for your own writing process.

## 🛠️ Managing your library

The application maintains a history of your searches. Click the History tab to see past queries. You can export these lists as a CSV file to open in Excel. This feature prevents you from searching for the same topic twice and makes it easier to organize your references.

## 🛡️ Privacy and safety

This tool functions as a bridge between your computer and public research databases. It does not store your personal information. It sends your search terms to the databases you select to get results. Please ensure you have appropriate access rights when downloading copyrighted material from commercial publishers.

## ❓ Troubleshooting common issues

### The application will not open
If the program does not start, restart your computer and try again. Ensure that no other antivirus software is blocking the application. Occasionally, Windows displays a security warning because the application is new. If you see this, click More Info and then select Run Anyway.

### Search results show no data
Check your internet connection first. If you are online, verify that you spelled your search terms correctly. Some databases place limits on how many searches you can perform in one hour. If you hit a limit, wait ten minutes before you try another search.

### Downloading fails
If a download fails, copy the link and try to open it in your web browser. If it works in the browser, allow the application a moment to reconnect and try again. High traffic on publisher websites can cause temporary connection drops.

Always credit original authors in your final documents.
